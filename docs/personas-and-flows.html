Personal Finance Manager

Phase 1 UX — Personas, Use Cases & Flows
Prepared for Harsh · June 4, 2026 · v0.1

This document sets up the human context for the Phase 1 design: who we are building for (personas), what they are trying to do (use cases), and how they move through the product step by step (flow diagrams). It precedes and informs the wireframes.

1. Personas

The product is household-first. The core unit is two adults sharing some finances, with an optional third member (e.g. an adult child or accountant). These personas recur throughout the flows.

P
Co-owner · Organizer

Priya, 38

The household "CFO." Sets up the account, invites the family, builds budgets, and checks in often.

Wants controlHigh engagement
  • Goals: one shared view, budgets that stay accurate, no surprises.
  • Frustrations: juggling logins, stale spreadsheets, nagging the family.
  • Needs: fast categorization, sub-categories, shared budgets, charts.
A
Co-owner · Casual

Arjun, 40

Priya's partner. Wants visibility with minimal effort, and privacy on his own account.

Low effortPrivacy-conscious
  • Goals: glance and trust the numbers; share what matters, hide the rest.
  • Frustrations: being forced into full transparency or fiddly setup.
  • Needs: easy invite-join, per-account privacy, balance-only option.
S
Member · Limited

Sam, 19

Adult child (or an accountant). Joins the household view but can't change household settings.

View-focusedOwn accounts only
  • Goals: see shared context, manage own account & spending.
  • Frustrations: being given too much or too little access.
  • Needs: member role, own-account control, simple dashboard.
